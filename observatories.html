Exploring the world's most powerful observatories and their cosmic discoveries.

Global Observatories

Astronomical observatories are the gateways to understanding the distant stars and galaxies.

Mauna Kea Observatory

Hawaii, USA

Located on a dormant volcano, it houses some of the world's largest optical, infrared, and submillimeter telescopes.

  • Discovery: First direct image of an exoplanet.

ALMA (Atacama Large Millimeter Array)

Atacama Desert, Chile

The most expensive ground-based telescope in operation, using 66 high-precision antennas to study the building blocks of stars.

  • Discovery: Detailed images of protoplanetary disks around young stars.

Yerkes Observatory

Wisconsin, USA

Known as the "birthplace of modern astrophysics," it houses the largest refracting telescope in the world.

  • Discovery: Early classification of star types and galaxy structures.

Space-Based Explorers

The James Webb & Hubble Duo

While ground observatories are limited by the atmosphere, space telescopes like Hubble and JWST provide the clearest views of the deep universe.

The JWST (James Webb Space Telescope) can see infrared light, allowing it to look back in time to the very first stars formed after the Big Bang.
