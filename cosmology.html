Cosmology

Understanding the Origin, Evolution, and Ultimate Fate of the Universe.

The Big Bang Theory

The Big Bang is the leading explanation for how the universe began. At its simplest, it says the universe as we know it started with a small singularity, then inflated over the next 13.8 billion years to the cosmos that we know today.

It wasn't an explosion in space; it was an expansion of space itself. In the first moments, the universe was incredibly hot and dense. As it expanded, it cooled down, allowing subatomic particles and eventually atoms to form.

Timeline of the Universe

0 Seconds: Singularity

The universe began as a point of infinite density and heat. Our current laws of physics cannot yet describe what happened at this exact moment.

10-32 Seconds: Inflation

The universe expanded from smaller than an atom to golf-ball size almost instantly, shaping early cosmic structure.

400 Million Years: First Stars

Gravity began to pull clouds of hydrogen and helium together. The "Cosmic Dark Ages" ended as the first stars ignited, creating the first light.

9 Billion Years: Our Sun

Our Solar System formed from a massive cloud of gas and dust. Gravity collapsed the center to form the Sun, while the remaining material formed the planets.

Evolution of Earth

Hadean Eon

4.6 Billion Years Ago. Earth was a molten ball of fire, frequently hit by asteroids. The Moon was likely formed during this time after a massive collision.

Archean Eon

The crust cooled, and water vapor in the atmosphere condensed to form the first oceans. Simple single-celled life began to emerge.

Phanerozoic Eon

Life became complex. Plants, dinosaurs, and eventually humans evolved as the continents shifted to their current positions.
